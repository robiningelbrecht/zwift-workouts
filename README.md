This repository contains **three structured Zwift workouts** designed to be used together as a simple, repeatable training program.  
The focus is on **aerobic development (Zone 2)** with **targeted intensity**.

The plan works well for riders training **4 or 5 days per week**, where the **Zone 2 ride is repeated multiple times**.

---

## Included Workouts

### 1. Over Under Threshold (50min)
**Purpose:** Build sustainable power just below FTP  
**Intensity:** High Zone 3 / Low Zone 4 (90‚Äì120% FTP)

This workout targets the ‚Äúcomfortably hard‚Äù range. It improves:
- Lactate clearance
- Muscular endurance
- Ability to hold steady power for longer efforts

Best done when relatively fresh.

---

### 2. 30/30 VO‚ÇÇmax (1h)
**Purpose:** Improve aerobic ceiling and repeatability  
**Intensity:** Very high (120‚Äì130% FTP during work intervals)

Short, sharp efforts with brief recoveries. This workout:
- Pushes VO‚ÇÇmax
- Improves ability to handle surges
- Trains fast power recruitment

This is the hardest workout of the week, treat it with respect.

---

### 3. Long Zone 2 Ride (1h30)
**Purpose:** Aerobic base & endurance  
**Intensity:** Zone 2 (60‚Äì70% FTP)

This is the foundation of the program. Zone 2 work:
- Improves mitochondrial density
- Builds fatigue resistance
- Supports recovery between harder days

This workout is intentionally repeatable and should feel **easy enough to finish while still feeling good**.

## How to Use These Workouts

1. Download the `.zwo` files
2. Place them in your Zwift workouts folder
    - **macOS:** `Documents/Zwift/Workouts/<your Zwift ID>/`
    - **Windows:** `Documents/Zwift/Workouts/<your Zwift ID>/`
3. Restart Zwift
4. Find the workouts under **Custom Workouts**

---

## Notes & Recommendations

- Make sure your **FTP is set correctly** in Zwift before starting.
- If fatigue accumulates, **drop one Zone 2 day**, not the hard workouts.
- This program is best suited for:
    - Endurance-focused riders
    - Riders building fitness over multiple weeks
    - Cyclists who prefer structure without complexity

These workouts are provided as-is and assume you are healthy and accustomed to structured training.
